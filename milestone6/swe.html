Software Transition

The spatial and analytical thinking required for drafting translates almost directly into coding and informs how I approach programming challenges today. Just as I once had to visualize how different building systems (electrical, plumbing, structural) would intersect in a physical space, I now think about how different modules of code interact within a program. The same attention to detail that ensured a door swing wouldn't interfere with a light switch in my drafts now helps me catch potential conflicts in program logic before they cause errors.

What excites me most about software engineering is the endless potential for discovery. I’ve embraced a "learn by doing" approach, whether experimenting with Linux commands to understand how operating systems function or watching how experienced developers structure their work. Every error message is a puzzle to solve, and every new concept unlocks another layer of possibility.
