Related Projects

Regarding Revit projects, one memorable assignment involved modeling a multi-story office building with coordinated systems. I created separate models for architectural, structural, and multiple components, then used Revit's collaboration tools to integrate them. Running clash detection revealed conflicts between ductwork and ceiling elements—a practical lesson in system integration that mirrors the dependency management I now encounter in software projects. Producing construction documents from the model demonstrated Revits power; when I adjusted a window size, the change propagated automatically to elevations, schedules, and detail sheets. This experience with live data shaped how I now think about relationships in web development.

While I primarily used AutoCAD at AC+Co, I trained in Revit for projects requiring three dimentional coordination. Unlike CAD’s layer-based system, Revit’s parametric modeling forced me to think in relationships (e.g., how a window insertion affects wall geometry).
